Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters. Here's exactly what data we collect, why, and how it's used.

Last updated: February 21, 2026

🔒 TL;DR: The IronRod desktop application collects zero data — no telemetry, no analytics, no network calls. This website uses Google Analytics and Google AdSense, which may collect anonymous usage data and serve personalized ads.

1. The IronRod Desktop Application

The IronRod desktop application (available for macOS, Windows, and Linux) is designed with privacy as a core principle:

  • No data collection: The app does not collect, transmit, or store any personal information.
  • No telemetry: No usage analytics, crash reports, or diagnostic data are sent anywhere.
  • No network access: The app operates entirely offline. It never connects to the internet.
  • Read-only scanning: The app opens your storage devices in strict read-only mode. It never writes to, modifies, or deletes data on your source drive.
  • Local processing: All file recovery, analysis, and repair happens locally on your machine. No data leaves your computer.

2. This Website

This website (ironrod-d3493.web.app) uses the following third-party services:

2.1 Firebase Hosting

This site is hosted on Google Firebase Hosting. Firebase may automatically collect:

  • IP address (for serving content and security)
  • Basic request logs (URL, timestamp, status code)

This data is managed by Google under the Google Privacy Policy.

2.2 Google Analytics (Firebase Analytics)

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with this website. This helps us improve the site and prioritize features. Analytics may collect:

  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Scroll depth and section visibility
  • Button clicks (downloads, navigation, FAQ interactions)
  • Device type, browser, operating system, and screen size
  • Approximate geographic location (country/region level, derived from IP)
  • Referral source (how you arrived at the site)

Analytics data is anonymous and aggregated. We do not collect names, email addresses, or any personally identifiable information through Analytics.

You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Google uses the DoubleClick cookie to serve ads. You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings or aboutads.info.
